Philippine officials have refused to call the current surge in the country's oil prices a "crisis." The data says otherwise.
At least in the capital region, Metro Manila, the average monthly price of diesel has soared an 35% since the start of the year, while that of gasoline (unleaded) rose 20% in the same period.
What's more, the increases were faster than those recorded in previous years when an oil price shock occurred, particularly due to events that started during the first three months of each year.
For instance, Russia's attack on Ukraine on Feb. 22, 2022 caused monthly average diesel and gasoline prices in Metro Manila to rise by 28% and 7%, respectively.
Average pump prices also went up much slower during the start of the Arab Spring protests that engulfed the oil-rich Middle East region in 2011. Meanwhile, declines below 20% were recorded during the first three months of 2020 when the Covid-19 pandemic started.
